PROTECT
YOURSELF
ONLINE

Prevention is the best defense. Learn concrete strategies to protect yourself, manage your digital footprint, and build a safer online environment for everyone.

01 —

4 Pillars of Cyberbullying Prevention

01
Control Your Digital Privacy

Control who can see your posts. Limit the personal information you share such as your address, phone number, school, and daily routine don't need to be on your public profile. Review privacy settings regularly on every platform you use.

02
Think Before You Post

Apply the "screenshot test", would you be embarrassed if this content were screenshotted and shared? What you upload to the internet can last forever. Avoid sharing content that could be misused against you later.

03
Build a Support Network

Maintain open communication with parents, teachers, or trusted adults. Talk about your online experiences regularly. A strong community is the best defense against the isolation that cyberbullies often target.

04
Be an Upstander, Not a Bystander

If you witness cyberbullying, don't stay silent. Support the victim directly or report the content. Active bystanders have great power to stop the cycle of online bullying before it escalates.

✓

✓

✓
Do not display your phone number or personal email on your public profile
✓
Google your own name to see what information is publicly accessible
Online Behavior 5 items
✓
Think about the long-term impact before uploading photos or videos of yourself
✓
Do not respond to provocations or negative comments emotionally
✓
Block and report disruptive accounts without feeling guilty
✓
Limit your time online and recognize signs of social media addiction
✓
Talk openly about your online experiences with your parents or guardian
Special Tip
For Students

Follow your school's technology use policy. Report cyberbullying incidents to a teacher or counselor — you don't have to face it alone. Schools have an obligation to protect you.

Special Tip
For Parents

Open regular dialogues about your child's online life without being judgmental. Learn the platforms they use. Establish device usage rules that are agreed upon together, not forced.

Special Tip
For Educators

Integrate digital literacy and digital citizenship into the curriculum. Create a safe classroom environment for reporting incidents. Be a role model for responsible technology use.
