Response Guide

HOW TO
RESPOND
BULLYING

When cyberbullying occurs, a proper and structured response is crucial. This step-by-step guide is designed for victims, witnesses, and parents who want to help.

01 —

Response Steps for Victims

Step 01 — Immediately
Don't Respond — Stop Contact

Your first instinct may be to respond or defend yourself, but this usually makes the situation worse. Perpetrators seek an emotional reaction. By not responding, you remove their "fuel".

Block the perpetrator's accounts on all platforms
Leave toxic groups or conversations
Turn off notifications temporarily to maintain calm

Step 02 — Documentation
Gather Evidence Before Deleting Anything

Evidence is the most important asset if you decide to report. Do not delete anything before documenting it.

Screenshot messages, posts, or comments along with date and time
Note the account name, profile URL, and platform used
Save evidence in a safe place — cloud storage or personal email
Write a chronological record of the incidents

Step 03 — Report
Use the Platform's Report Feature

All social media platforms have reporting mechanisms. Use this feature — it is your right, and violating content will be acted upon.

Report the account/content using the "Report" button
Select the most appropriate category (harassment, hate speech, etc.)
Include evidence or a clear description when reporting
Save the report reference number if available

Step 04 — Tell Someone
Contact a Trusted Adult or Authority

Cyberbullying is not shameful and it is not your fault. Sharing with a trusted person is an important step — you do not have to carry this alone.

Tell your parents, teacher, or school counselor
If it involves physical threats, report to the police with the evidence you have gathered
Consider consulting a mental health professional

Step 05 — Recover
Guard Your Mental Health & Limit Exposure

Recovery is a process, not an event. Take time to intentionally take care of yourself.

Take a break from social media — this is not giving up, it is being wise
Spend time with people who support you positively
Do activities that make you feel capable and happy
If symptoms of anxiety or depression appear, seek professional help immediately

When Should You Report to the Police Immediately?

Report to authorities if cyberbullying involves: direct physical threats, extortion or blackmail, sharing intimate content without consent (revenge porn), or stalking that makes you feel unsafe.

In Indonesia, these actions can be reported to the National Police via lapor.go.id or by visiting the nearest police station. Bring all the evidence you have documented. You can also report to KOMINFO for content that violates the ITE Law.

02 —

Guide by Role

If You Are a
Witness / Bystander
  • Don't like, share, or comment on bullying content
  • Send a private message to the victim to show support
  • Report the content to the platform
  • Inform an adult who can help
  • Encourage other friends to do the same — there is strength in numbers
If You Are a
Parent / Guardian
  • Listen without judging — resist the urge to immediately "fix" things
  • Believe your child's story without questioning its truth
  • Help document evidence together
  • Contact the school and if necessary, law enforcement
  • Seek counseling support for your child and yourself
If You Are a
Teacher / Counselor
  • Handle reports seriously and confidentially
  • Involve the parents of all parties involved
  • Apply the school's anti-bullying policy consistently
  • Provide psychological support to the victim
  • Document all actions taken in writing
