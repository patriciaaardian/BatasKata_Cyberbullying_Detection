WHAT IS
CYBER
BULLYING?

Understanding cyberbullying is the first step to fighting it. Learn the definition, criteria, and various forms you need to recognize to protect yourself and the people around you.

DEF
INI
TION § 01 — Definition

"The use of digital technology to harm, threaten, humiliate, or exclude someone repeatedly."

Cyberbullying is a form of bullying that occurs through digital devices such as smartphones, computers, and tablets — including social media, messaging platforms, online games, and internet forums. Unlike conventional bullying, cyberbullying can happen anytime and anywhere, even in spaces that should be safe, like home.

Under Indonesia's ITE Law, actions that spread content containing insults, defamation, or threats through digital platforms may be subject to criminal sanctions. Cyberbullying is not just a social problem — it is a legal matter.

02 —

What Counts as Cyberbullying?

Repeated

Happens more than once. A single incident may be just that, but a repeated pattern indicates cyberbullying.

Intentional

The perpetrator is aware and intends to harm the victim. Accidents or misunderstandings generally do not fall into this category.

Power Imbalance

There is a difference in power — such as popularity, age, follower count, or technical ability — between the perpetrator and the victim.

Through Technology

Occurs on digital platforms: social media, text messages, email, online games, forums, or other digital spaces.

Causes Harm

Has a negative impact on the victim's mental, emotional, social, or even physical well-being.

Publicly Witnessed

Often carried out publicly or witnessed by an audience, which worsens the victim's sense of shame.
